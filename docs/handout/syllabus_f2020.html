Course Description: Introductory treatment of the fundamental, unifying concepts of the mechanics of continua with applications to classical solid and fluid mechanics.

Definition of a Credit Hour: Success in this 3 credit hour course is based on the expectation that students will spend, for each unit of credit, a minimum of 45 hours over the length of the course (normally 3 hours per unit per week with 1 of the hours used for lecture) for instruction and preparation/studying or course related activities for a total of 135 hours.

Measurable Student Learning Outcomes: Upon successful completion of this course, students will be able to

  • Derive equations governing the mechanics of continua
  • Analyze stress and strain states for large deformation problems
  • Calculate the response in a viscoelastic body
  • Analyze stress and strain in an anisotropic body

Course Textbook: The textbook used in this class is: Introduction to Continuum Mechanics, W. Lai, 4th Ed.

Other References: For further study, the following supplemental texts are recommended

  • A.J.M. Spencer, Continuum Mechanics
  • G.E. Mase, Schaum’s Outline of Continuum Mechanics
  • Y.C. Fung, A First Course in Continuum Mechanics
